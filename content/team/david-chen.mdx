David K. Chen leads Justice & Partners' Intellectual Property and Technology Law practice. With a graduate degree in computer science from MIT and over 25 years of IP litigation experience, David offers clients a rare combination of technical depth and legal acumen that is essential in today's innovation-driven economy.

## Practice Focus

David represents technology companies, inventors, and entrepreneurs in all aspects of intellectual property law — from patent prosecution at the USPTO through complex multi-patent federal litigation. He has successfully litigated patent disputes in the ITC and district courts nationwide, negotiated landmark IP licensing agreements, and conducted IP due diligence for transactions valued at over $500 million.

His clients range from individual inventors and pre-revenue startups to publicly traded technology companies protecting their core innovations.

## Notable Matters

- Lead counsel in a patent infringement case that resulted in a $47M judgment for a medical device company
- Secured a preliminary injunction protecting a client's trade secrets against misappropriation by a former employee and a competitor
- Advised a Series C SaaS company on the licensing and commercialisation of its AI-driven platform IP
